About London

If you are reading this you are probably heading to London for the first time ever. This might even be your first time travelling by yourself! Yes, that might sound scary and your mind is racing all over the place trying to figure out where to stay, where to eat, what to do, etc. Well, you came to the right site! Here I will tell you my own experinces on travelling in London for the first time and give you some useful tips on how to maneuver this wonderful city! Hopefully at the end of reading this site you have a solid plan on what to do in London and this becomes one of the best trips of your life!

But before diving into the main content we have to learn a little bit about London and how it came about. London is the capital and largest city of England and the United Kingdom. It is considered to be one of the world's most important global citites. It has been termed the world's most powerful, most desirable, most influential, most visited, most expensive, innovative, sustainable, most investment friendly, and most popular for work city in the world. With a population reaching 9 million people it is one of the fastest growing cities in the world and welcomes around 30 million visitors every year!

Some quick facts about London is that the city was founded around 2000 years ago by the Romans. London uses the pound sterling as its currency. The weather is mostly cloudy and can vary over the year. The best time to visit London is the summer, during this time the temperature is in the high 70's and 80's. It is great weather to walk around!

Where to stay?!

London is a big city with many different neighborhoods for different kinds of people. Some neighborhoods are more central and convenient than others, given that most visitors to London walk or use public transport to reach the attractions. Luckily London has a great public transportation system that runs every day! Fun fact; London locals call the subway "the tube!" London has no downtown area, everything is spread out and each neighborhood has a unique sense to it. The West End (Covent Garden, Soho, Leicester Square, Oxford Street, Mayfair) is a very central district with most of London’s theatres, top art galleries, varied dining for all budgets and much of London’s nightlife. 4-star and 5-star hotels dominate, with some midrange bargains. This area is excellent for families staying in London. Just north of the West End, Bloomsbury and Fitzrovia are also centrally located, walkable to West End, with lots of bookshops, quiet streets, and the British Museum as the star attraction. A wide range of accommodation, cheaper than the neighboring West End. King’s Cross, with its train stations, is north of Fitzrovia. It’s low on sights, but has excellent transport links to other parts of London, to Heathrow and Gatwick airports and to France and Belgium via the Eurostar. Mostly inexpensive guesthouses and chain hotels. When I was in London I stayed for a short amount of time and I stayed in a hostel. Hostels are very common in London and are very cheap, some are as cheap as $50 a night. I stayed in a hostel that was right on top of a pub! It was a great atmosphere and I had no complaints! Good thing about London is that you can stay anywhere in the city and you will be near a tube station. This will make it easier to get around in a quick and timely manor!

Where and what to eat!

With London being a melting pot of cultures, this means that you will be able to try some of the worlds best cuisine in just one city. Whether it is eating mac and cheese in Camden Market or treating youself to some high-end cuisine in SoHo.London has options for anyone and everyone on any kind of budget. London's food scene is fantastic these days and with all the options the city has to offer it can get overwhelming. Well look no further I am here to tell you the top five must-visit restaurats you have to visit while in London.

Top 5 restaurants in London!

  2. Poppies in Camden
  3. Duck and Waffle in Whitechapel
  4. Pastation on Warren Street
  5. The Mac Factory in Camden Town
